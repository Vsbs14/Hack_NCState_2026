How I Fixed My Leaky Kitchen Faucet

February 8, 2026 · Dave Morrison · 4 min read

Last Saturday I noticed a steady drip from the kitchen faucet that had been getting worse over the past week. Rather than call a plumber, I decided to take a crack at it myself. I'd never done faucet repair before, but it turned out to be way more straightforward than I expected.

Our faucet is a Moen single-handle pull-down model that's about seven years old. A quick search told me the most likely culprit was a worn-out cartridge. Moen makes replacement cartridges that are pretty easy to find at any hardware store.

What I needed

  • Moen 1225 replacement cartridge ($24 at the local hardware store)
  • Adjustable wrench
  • Flat-head screwdriver
  • Plumber's grease
  • Old towel

The process

First thing: turn off the water supply valves under the sink. I also turned on the faucet to release any remaining pressure. Then I put the towel in the sink to catch small parts.

Removing the handle was the trickiest part, mainly because the set screw was hidden under a small decorative cap. I used the flat-head screwdriver to gently pry it off. Once the screw was out, the handle lifted right off.

The retaining nut came off with the adjustable wrench. Then I pulled the old cartridge straight up and out. You could see the wear on the rubber seals — that was definitely the source of the leak.

I applied a thin layer of plumber's grease to the new cartridge, slid it in, and reassembled everything in reverse order. The whole thing took about 35 minutes, including the time I spent watching a YouTube video to make sure I was doing it right.

One thing to watch out for: make sure you orient the new cartridge the same way as the old one. There's a small tab that needs to line up with a notch in the faucet body. If it's wrong, the hot and cold will be swapped.

Result

No more drip. Honestly feels good to have fixed something around the house without having to pay someone $150+ for what ended up being a pretty simple repair. The cartridge has a limited lifetime warranty from Moen too, so if it fails again they'll replace it free.

If your Moen faucet is dripping, I'd say give it a try before calling a plumber. Just take your time with the set screw and you'll be fine.
